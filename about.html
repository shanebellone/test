About Me

My founder’s journey spans 10+ years. After leaving the military, I earned an undergraduate degree in communications. I immediately joined a graduate program where I focused on persuasive and political communication. I did not finish the latter, which amusingly, makes me both a high school and graduate school dropout. In both cases, tedium led to disinterest. I am not competitive, nor do I desire fortune. My only persisting desire is to build a business that outlives me.

For several years, I pursued passive revenue. Unfortunately, I lacked the coding skills necessary to produce a product with substantial utility. I searched for profits without considering how I might add value. All transactional relationships represent an exchange of value, and all relationships are transactional. This realization changed my trajectory.

In 2017, I resolved to change that. By 2019, I began coding daily. Today, I have the tools required to build a business that serves its customers with meaningful value.

Pathview

Pathview is a product dictated by external constraints in a market dominated by large companies who use the same general approach. Analytics is a wonderful and complicated problem with dissatisfied users awaiting a fresh solution.

Pathview is a content analytics application focused on cookieless event-tracking that intends to answer 4 simple questions:

  1. Where am I?
  2. What am I doing right?
  3. What am I doing wrong?
  4. What should I do next?

The answers to these questions are actionable. Despite this, I have never left an analytics’ dashboard feeling confident. This essential feedback loop has always felt broken.

Pathview intends to fix this. The application handles data as an intentional business outcome, rather than a byproduct. Web data provides actionable insight that can improve products, services, and — most critically — the user experience.

The application records and displays relevant key performance indicators alongside the full user path for each recorded event. Pathview does not use cookies, personal data, or track visitors across multiple websites. The company believes visitors have a right to privacy. Pathview cares about improving the user experience, not about attributing an experience to an individual.

It is vital for a business’s interests to align with their customers’. Pathview will rely upon its own analytics to grow. There is no better way to improve a product than to be its first and most dependent user. I hope this will lead to rapid feature development and many quality-of-life updates over the years.

We can fix our failures only after identifying them. Pathview will help lead the search.
